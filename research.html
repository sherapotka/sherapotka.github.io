Research Statement

Ethical AI Systems

I design AI systems that are technically robust, ethically grounded, and socially responsible, with a focus on fairness, bias mitigation, and privacy in NLP and recommender systems.

My work integrates algorithmic innovation with critical reflection on social impacts, ensuring models optimize performance while upholding equity, transparency, and accountability. By combining computational experiments with insights from computer science, ethics, and the social sciences, I address the dual challenge of robustness and trustworthiness. My broader goal is to develop methods that mitigate systemic harms while preserving utility, enabling responsible AI deployment in real-world contexts.

My work blends rigorous experiments with socially informed objectives so that deployed AI respects diverse human experiences.

Highlights
  • FairRec: Developed fairness-aware link recommendation algorithms that ensure exposure parity, improving equitable visibility of minority groups in social recommender systems.
  • Bias Auditing: Conducted large-scale bias evaluations across GPT, BERT, Cohere, and BGE using Fightin’ Words and SC-WEAT, providing both statistical rigor and interpretability in bias detection.
  • Weighted Bias Scoring: Introduced a novel scoring framework inspired by the Large Language Model Bias Index (LLMBI) to capture nuanced disparities across demographic dimensions.
  • Differential Privacy (DP): Designed DP-based text generation methods with embedding-space perturbations, balancing the trade-off between privacy protection and semantic utility.
  • Cross-Domain Relevance: Applied fairness and privacy methodologies across both NLP and recommender systems, demonstrating the adaptability and scalability of my approaches in diverse AI contexts.

1) Fairness in Recommendation Systems

Recommendation algorithms shape visibility. FairRec optimizes both accuracy and equitable exposure by integrating exposure-parity constraints into the objective over a probabilistic graph model.

  • Addresses homophily and popularity bias; boosts minority visibility.
  • Constrained optimization + stochastic sampling scales to large graphs.
  • On Twitter & citation graphs: +35% visibility for under-represented users with no significant precision loss.

2) Demographic Bias Detection in Large Language Models

I quantify and explain demographic biases in embeddings from GPT/BERT/Cohere/BGE using a frequency-weighted Fightin’ Words variant and SC-WEAT for contextual effects.

  • Computes interpretable z-scores to reveal group-linked word associations.
  • Findings show persistent links (e.g., “Black” → criminal terms; “female” → domesticity/passivity) across models.
  • Results inform auditing and debiasing for production LLMs.

3) Privacy-Preserving Text Generation

I develop differentially private generation by perturbing embedding space rather than token probabilities, preserving coherence while reducing memorization and leakage.

  • DP-SGD plus inference-time embedding noise for smooth, contextual outputs.
  • −60% identifiable memorization with >85% human-rated quality retained.
  • Applications: healthcare narratives, legal anonymization, educational feedback.
